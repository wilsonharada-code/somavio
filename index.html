If you encounter a bear
🐻
Stay calm — do not run
Running triggers the bear's pursuit instinct. Stand your ground and assess the situation.
📢
Speak calmly, wave your arms
Let the bear identify you as human, not prey. Talk in a calm, firm voice. Avoid direct eye contact.
👥
Back away slowly — stay in groups
Never turn your back. Move sideways or diagonally. Groups are safer — bears rarely approach groups of 4 or more.
🫙
Use bear spray if bear is under 10 metres
Deploy in a wide arc at ground level. Aim slightly downward, into the bear's face and path. Do not use as insect repellent — it attracts bears when applied to clothing or gear.
📵
Do not photograph or approach
Maintain at least 100 metres distance at all times. This is Parks Canada regulation, not a suggestion.
Bear spray — what you need to know
✅
Legal and recommended in Canada
Bear spray is legal to carry and use for wildlife deterrence across Canada. Parks Canada recommends it on all backcountry trails.
⚠️
Not the same as pepper spray
Bear spray uses a different formulation and concentration. Pepper spray is classified as a prohibited weapon in Canada and cannot be carried.
✈️
Cannot be brought across international borders
Bear spray purchased abroad will be confiscated at Canadian customs. Purchase in Canmore before your hike.
Available in Canmore: Mountain Equipment (MEC), Sport Chek, Canadian Tire, most outdoor gear shops. Cost: approximately CAD $40–60. Practice deploying the safety clip before your hike.
Before you head out today
Check current trail status before leaving Conditions may have changed since this alert was issued — verify at albertaparks.ca
Tell someone your route and expected return time If you don't return, someone needs to know where to look
Carry bear spray and know how to use it Practice removing the safety clip before you go — not during an encounter
Hike in groups of 4 or more when possible Bears rarely approach larger groups — especially important during active wildlife alerts
Download offline maps before heading out Cell signal is unreliable on most trails in this area
Emergency: call 911 Parks Canada Dispatch (24/7): 1-888-773-8888
